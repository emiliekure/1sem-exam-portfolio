Global goal campaign

Flow 4 project

Scope

Acting as one of the stores located in Lyngby Storcenter, we were to create a landingpage for a campaign based on one or more of the UN global goals, for which we had to produce text, graphics in Illustrator and a video in Premiere Pro. The two main components of the project is the landingpage and the video. The project was a group effort.

Full project description

Initial idea

Our group was pretty quick to settle on The Body Shop as our store, because they already have a lot of focus on the environment in their company mission. A big part of their product packaging is zero waste and can be recycled, and their products are vegan as well, which is why we decided that our campaign would focus on UN global goals number 12 which deals with "responsible consumption and production". As such, our campaign is not promoting a new concept, but rather focusing on the steps the company is already taking in direction of our chosen goal and setting it in the context of Christmas and The Body Shop's Christmas line of products.

Challenges and process

Our idea for the campaign video was to focus on how global warming has decreased the chances of a white Christmas happening in Denmark, and how The Body Shop works to reverse or at least decrease the effects of global warming "to get the traditional white Christmas back". The video would feature two people attempting and failing to do snow activities because of the obvious lack of snow. As they give up and return home, they watch as a third person litters a plastic bottle, making our main characters angry at the contribution to global warming. They then dispose of the bottle correctly and the video cuts to product shots of The Body Shop's Winter Jasmine line covered in snow. We wrote out what message we wanted to convey and used it as our voiceover to ensure that the message was clear to the audience. We wanted to base our landingpage on The Body Shop's website, and looked in their style guide as well as their website code to get the right colours and fonts. The header would simply feature the stores logo and a link back to the main page, and the footer was a dummy copy of The Body Shop's own without working links. We went with two simple icons for our graphics to emphasise the focus of the campaign, and a short explanatory text to keep the video at the center of attention.

Feedback

Due to corona we weren't able to get feedback on our landingpages, but one of our group members discussed it with a professor afterwards. The feedback here was that our body text could be smaller, and that the background colour could match the light beige/yellow that were used for text on the dark green backgrounds. Additionally the headlines could be larger, and the different elements could be made smaller, to create more space on the page.
