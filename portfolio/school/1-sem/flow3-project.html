Build an app

Flow 3 project

Scope

In groups, acting as a company or organisation of our choice, we were to make a prototype of an app in Adobe XD, based on one or more of the UN global goals. The goal(s) should play an essential role in the product value of the app.

Full project description

Initial idea

Our group were very intrigued with UN global goal number 12 which deals with "responsible consumption and production" as well as numbers 14 and 15 which are about protecting and conserving life below water and on land, respectively. We came up with a concept in which the users of the app, would be rewarded for using sustainable alternatives to disposable products like the to-go coffee cups from the bakery, or the chopsticks from your sushi place. We also felt that our concept could impact goal number 13 concerning "actions to combat climate change". We then decided that it would make sense with our concept to act as the organisation Dansk Retursystem, who runs the Danish deposit system.

Challenges and process

We wanted to incorporate the three main colours of our organisation (blue, green and grey) on our app subpages, and we brainstormed that we would need 5 subpages, which meant we needed two more colours to have a separate colour for each section. On the Dansk Retursystem website, they had used orange for one of their sections which we also decided to use, as well as a dark grey of our own choosing. We then realised that we needed an additinal colour for the login and front sections of the app, for which we chose light green. We wanted to keep the look simple which is why we chose a single font and didn't use any borders or dividing lines.

Product

The concept of the app is to be able to register which sustainable alternatives to disposable and plastic products you own. The user creates a profile in which they register the owned products, and stores that offer to-go food and drinks are able to list which of your products you are able to use as a substitute for their disposable product. Whenever the user chooses to do so, they can register the substitution in the app through a QR-code, and are in turn rewarded with points. These points can then be used in a prize shop, in exchange for sustainable products such as to-go cups and chopsticks to use with the app, but also products in for example the beauty/hygiene and kitchen storage categories.

Feedback

We found that people thought there were too many colours that seemed random, which made sense because a prospective user would not necessarily have knowledge of Dansk Retursystem prior to using the app. It also wasn't very clear what the icons in our bottom menu signified, because they hadn't been associated with the specific sections beforehand. Additionally there was a concern that the app didn't feature enough explanatory text to convey the concept.
